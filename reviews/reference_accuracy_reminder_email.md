Dear Authors,

A focused reminder before the May 12, 2026 camera-ready deadline: ACL is enforcing a zero-tolerance policy on hallucinated, fabricated, or misattributed references this year. **A paper in the proceedings found with such references will be removed from the proceedings.** This is a hard reject criterion, not a soft warning.

Before you submit, please verify every reference in your bibliography against the actual source. For each citation, check:

- The cited paper exists at the venue and year you list.
- The authors and title match the source exactly.
- The claim attributed to the citation in your prose matches what the source actually says.

If you used any LLM assistance during writing, reference fabrication is the highest-risk failure mode. Manual verification of every reference is essential, even for citations you believe are correct.

## Recommended Tool (Optional)

[RefChecker](https://github.com/...), an open-source reference verification tool, can speed up the check by automatically validating citations against external sources. Using the tool is optional; the accuracy requirement is mandatory regardless of how you verify.

## Scope

The reference accuracy requirement applies most strictly to archival papers, which enter the ACL Anthology and are subject to ACL's reference policy. **We expect accuracy from all accepted submissions, archival or non-archival.**
